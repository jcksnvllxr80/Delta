**Gameplay Concept for *Delta*** 🎮

*Delta* is an open-world adventure game where the player explores a large, mysterious land one screen at a time. The world is arranged as a **grid of interconnected screens**, allowing the player to move north, south, east, or west into new areas. From the very beginning, the player can wander almost anywhere, encouraging exploration and discovery rather than a fixed path.

The player begins with minimal equipment and must **explore the overworld to find tools, weapons, and hidden locations**. Many areas contain **secret caves**, merchants, or upgrades that are not immediately visible. Some secrets may be hidden behind destructible walls, concealed entrances, or environmental puzzles that reward experimentation.

Throughout the world are several **puzzle-based dungeons**, each containing enemies, traps, and locked rooms. Inside these dungeons, the player must solve spatial puzzles, defeat monsters, and locate keys in order to progress deeper. Each dungeon contains a **special item** that not only helps complete that dungeon but also unlocks new possibilities in the overworld, allowing the player to access areas that were previously unreachable.

Combat is real-time and occurs directly in the overworld and dungeons. The player must **avoid enemy attacks while striking with weapons or tools**, gradually becoming stronger by finding better equipment and increasing health.

A central design goal for *Delta* is **player-driven discovery**. The world does not explicitly guide the player; instead, clues, environmental hints, and experimentation reveal the path forward. Players might find shortcuts, hidden passages, or powerful items long before they are expected to.

Progression occurs by completing dungeons and gathering important artifacts. Each completed dungeon brings the player closer to uncovering the central mystery of the world and confronting the final challenge.

Overall, *Delta* is designed to feel like a **mysterious world full of secrets**, where exploration, experimentation, and clever puzzle solving are just as important as combat.
