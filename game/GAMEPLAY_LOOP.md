### Gameplay Progression Loop for *Delta* 🔺

The progression system in *Delta* is built around a **discovery → preparation → challenge → reward** cycle that constantly feeds the player new tools and new mysteries.

---

## 1. Exploration Phase

The player begins by **wandering the overworld**.

During this phase the player:

* Finds caves, hidden shops, and secret areas
* Discovers entrances to dungeons
* Collects upgrades like health, currency, or equipment
* Encounters obstacles they **cannot yet solve**

Examples of blockers:

* Rivers that require a crossing tool
* Heavy stones that require lifting power
* Burnable trees hiding caves
* Cracked walls that can be destroyed

These blockers create **curiosity hooks** that encourage the player to remember locations and return later.

---

## 2. Dungeon Discovery

Once a dungeon is found, the player enters a **self-contained puzzle labyrinth**.

Inside a dungeon the player:

* Navigates rooms filled with enemies and traps
* Finds keys to unlock deeper rooms
* Solves spatial or environmental puzzles
* Gradually uncovers the dungeon layout

A dungeon is designed so that the player **cannot fully complete it without discovering a key item within it**.

---

## 3. Item Acquisition

Midway through each dungeon the player finds a **unique tool or ability**.

Examples of dungeon items:

* A ladder to cross water gaps
* Bombs to destroy cracked walls
* A candle to burn hidden paths
* A grappling tool to cross pits

This item serves **two purposes**:

1. It solves puzzles in the current dungeon.
2. It unlocks **previously blocked areas in the overworld**.

This creates the classic **“aha” moment** where players realize where the item can be used elsewhere.

---

## 4. Dungeon Completion

Using the newly acquired item, the player reaches the **boss chamber**.

Boss encounters are designed so that:

* The dungeon item often provides the **key strategy** to defeating the boss.
* The boss tests whether the player truly understands the mechanic introduced in that dungeon.

Defeating the boss rewards:

* A major progression artifact
* Increased health or strength
* Story advancement

---

## 5. World Expansion

After leaving the dungeon, the world **opens up further**.

Previously unreachable areas now become accessible because the player has:

* New traversal abilities
* New combat options
* Increased durability

This sends the player back into the **exploration phase**, but now with more capability.

---

# The Core Gameplay Loop

The loop looks like this:

```
Explore Overworld
      ↓
Discover Blockers & Dungeons
      ↓
Enter Dungeon
      ↓
Solve Puzzles / Fight Enemies
      ↓
Find Dungeon Item
      ↓
Use Item to Reach Boss
      ↓
Defeat Boss & Gain Power
      ↓
Unlock New Overworld Areas
      ↓
Explore Again
```

Then the cycle repeats with **bigger dungeons and tougher bosses**.

---

# How the Loop Keeps Players Interested

### Curiosity

Players constantly encounter things they **cannot solve yet**, creating long-term intrigue.

### Discovery

Hidden caves, shortcuts, and secrets reward experimentation.

### Power Growth

Each dungeon makes the player feel noticeably stronger.

### Expanding World

The map never feels finished — every new ability reveals **more of the world**.

### Puzzle Variety

Each dungeon introduces **a new mechanic**, preventing repetition.

### Memory & Return

Players remember strange locations and later return with the correct tool, creating satisfying payoffs.

---

✔ The result is a **self-propelling exploration loop** where curiosity leads to discovery, discovery leads to power, and power leads to deeper exploration.

---

The design rule is a simple dungeon progression pattern that makes the loop addictive.
